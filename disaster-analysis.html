Disaster Analysis and Response Tool

Project Information

  • Project Type: Team Competition
  • Team: Chamaru Amasara, Sasmitha Manathunga, Sandil Ranasinghe
  • Competition: IFS x Hatch ChallengerX AI / ML Incubator
  • Project Duration: November 2022 - May 2023
  • Achievements: 2nd Place Runners Up

As part of the IFS Hatch ChallengerX incubator, I was part of a team Model.fit that developed DART - Disaster Analysis and Response Tool. This tool is a predictive modelling system that helps organizations to maximize their readiness for natural disasters. The tool works by incorporating geographical and workforce assets with artificial neural networks (ANNs) for each disaster type. This allows the tool to model the impact of a potential disaster on an organization's assets and workforce, and to recommend the best response strategy. The tool is designed to be easy to use, with a user-friendly interface. DART offers automated data preprocessing, feature engineering, and predictive model-building pipelines that provide graphical insights, helping clients predict damage to their assets and mitigate potential losses. We harnessed the power of cutting-edge technologies such as React, Python, PyTorch, FastAPI, Postgres, and Esri ArcGIS to power DART, making it a game-changing product. The ChallengerX Incubator program allowed us to meet and learn from industry leaders and experts from various fields, including senior executives and engineers from IFS. The experience was truly enlightening, as we gained a profound understanding of how global-scale digital products function as well as providing us with an avenue to solve real-world problems. We also received the incredible opportunity to use the Hatch Co-Working space, which allowed us to connect with many entrepreneurs and outstanding individuals.
